Dave(Hyeonjung)Kim

Ph.D. Student | ROBOTICS @ UCLA

Incoming Mechanical Engineering Ph.D. student at UCLA, joining Prof. Clites’ Anatomics Lab as a Waldron Robotics Fellow. I am broadly interested in bioinspired robot design, dexterous manipulation, prosthetics and learning-based control. My goal is to build robots that genuinely help people by coupling smart mechanical design with modern learning methods, whether through humanoids, prosthetics or assistive devices.

Before UCLA, I worked on the Jump Enhancing Textile-Suit at Yonsei’s HCAIR Lab, tendon-driven prosthetic hands and the Dual-Transmission Actuator at SNU’s Biorobotics Lab, and sim-to-real of IMU-free bipedal humanoids at Yonsei Artificial Intelligence. Across these projects I have worked the full stack from mechanical design to low-level control to learning-based approaches, with hands-on experience in SolidWorks, PyTorch, IsaacLab, MATLAB, and Dynamixel. I think of myself as a roboticist who is equally at home building hardware and training policies. Expecting to work on design and control of humanoid hands suited for human interaction during my doctoral stay at UCLA.

Advanced Intelligent Systems · 2025
A Soft Wearable Robot for Vertical Jump Enhancement via a Pneumatic Energy-Storing Propulsion Actuator and Triarticular Kinetic-Chained Structure
Sunghun Kim, Hyeokjun Seo, Hyeonjung Kim, Sengryeol Lee, Namho Kim, Dongjun Shin
We present the Jump Enhancing Textile (JET)-Suit, a soft wearable exoskeleton that increases vertical jump height through a pneumatic energy-storing actuator (PESPA) and a triarticular kinetic-chained structure (TKiCS).
DOI
We present the Jump Enhancing Textile (JET)-Suit, a soft wearable exoskeleton that increases vertical jump height through a pneumatic energy-storing actuator (PESPA) and a triarticular kinetic-chained structure (TKiCS). Across 12 participants, the suit achieved an average 3.74 cm and maximum 5.52 cm increase in unassisted jump height, validated through a multi-session human subject study with subject-specific open-loop control calibration.
