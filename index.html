Dave(Hyeonjung)Kim

Ph.D. Student | ROBOTICS @ UCLA

I'm an incoming Mechanical Engineering Ph.D. student at UCLA with a focus on Robotics and Systems & Control, where I am fortunate to be advised by Dr. Tyler Clites. My Ph.D. is supported by the Waldron Robotics Fellowship. My research focuses on bioinspired robotics, dexterous manipulation and prosthetics. A central question of my Ph.D. is how compliant joint architectures, tendon-like actuation, and impedance-aware learned control can be co-designed for robots that are both dexterous and natural during contact.

During my undergraduate studies, I have worked on the Jump Enhancing Textile (JET)-Suit at Yonsei’s HCAIR Lab, tendon-driven prosthetic hands and the Dual-Transmission Actuator at SNU’s Biorobotics Lab, and sim-to-real of IMU-free bipedal humanoids at Yonsei Artificial Intelligence. Across these projects I have worked the full stack from mechanical design to low-level control to learning-based approaches, with hands-on experience in SolidWorks, PyTorch, IsaacLab, MATLAB.

Advanced Intelligent Systems · 2025
A Soft Wearable Robot for Vertical Jump Enhancement via a Pneumatic Energy-Storing Propulsion Actuator and Triarticular Kinetic-Chained Structure
Sunghun Kim, Hyeokjun Seo, Hyeonjung Kim, Sengryeol Lee, Namho Kim, Dongjun Shin
We present the Jump Enhancing Textile (JET)-Suit, a soft wearable exoskeleton that helps increases user's vertical jump height through a pneumatic energy-storing actuator (PESPA) and a triarticular kinetic-chained structure (TKiCS).
DOI
We present the Jump Enhancing Textile (JET)-Suit, a soft wearable exoskeleton that increases vertical jump height through a pneumatic energy-storing actuator (PESPA) and a triarticular kinetic-chained structure (TKiCS). Across 12 participants, the suit achieved an average 3.74 cm and maximum 5.52 cm increase in unassisted jump height, validated through a multi-session human subject study with subject-specific open-loop control calibration.
